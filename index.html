Dr. Zakir Abdul Karim Naik

The Voice of Comparative Religion and Peaceful Dialogue

Here's a time line of Dr. Zakir Naik's life:

1965 - Born in Mumbai, India
Dr. Zakir Naik was born on October 18, 1965, in Mumbai (formerly Bombay), India. He later went on to study medicine and earned a Bachelor of Medicine and Surgery (MBBS) degree. However, despite being a qualified doctor, Dr. Naik found his calling in Islamic studies, which led him to focus on religious education and preaching.
1991 - Founded the Islamic Research Foundation (IRF)
In 1991, Dr. Naik founded the Islamic Research Foundation (IRF), a non-profit organization dedicated to spreading knowledge about Islam. The IRF aimed to clear misconceptions about Islam, promote peace, and enhance interfaith understanding. It produced religious lectures, debates, and educational programs in several languages, including English, Urdu, and Hindi.
1994 - Begins hosting public talks and debates
By the mid-1990s, Dr. Naik became known for his public lectures on Islam and comparative religion. He was unique in his approach of using logic, reason, and scientific interpretations to address misconceptions about Islam. He also regularly engaged in debates on topics like Christianity, Hinduism, and atheism, emphasizing the similarities between Islam and other major religions.
1996 - Launches the popular TV show "Peace TV"
In 1996, Dr. Naik launched Peace TV, a satellite television network that broadcasts Islamic content globally. The channel reached over 100 million viewers, becoming one of the leading platforms for Islamic educational content. Dr. Naik used this platform to broadcast his lectures, question-and-answer sessions, and interfaith debates, which garnered attention from Muslims and non-Muslims alike.
2000 - Public debates gain recognition
Dr. Naik became internationally recognized for his ability to debate with religious scholars from different faiths, especially on the topic of comparative religion. His debates, particularly with Christian theologians, Hindu scholars, and atheists, were broadcast worldwide, gaining a large following. He emphasized finding common ground between Islam and other faiths, promoting interfaith dialogue.
2005 - Receives the King Faisal International Prize
Dr. Naik's contributions to Islamic knowledge and education were acknowledged in 2005 when he was awarded the King Faisal International Prize for Service to Islam. This prestigious award recognized his work in spreading the message of Islam through peaceful means and his efforts to promote understanding and tolerance between different religions.
2007 - Ranked among the "100 Most Influential People in India"
In 2007, Dr. Naik was featured in the Indian Express as one of the "100 Most Influential People in India." His public appearances, lectures, and debates had a significant impact, particularly on youth, both in India and globally. His ability to present Islam in a scientific, logical manner attracted people from diverse religious backgrounds.
2010 - Delivers his first public talk in the UK at Wembley Arena
In June 2010, Dr. Naik delivered a public lecture at Wembley Arena in London, which was attended by thousands of people. This marked one of his first public appearances in the UK and further solidified his reputation as a prominent Islamic speaker. He spoke on topics such as Islamic teachings, interfaith dialogue, and science in the Qur’an, and engaged in an extensive Q&A session with the audience.
2012 - Receives the Dubai International Holy Quran Award
In 2012, Dr. Naik was honored with the Dubai International Holy Quran Award for his outstanding contributions to Islamic preaching. His global reach through lectures, Peace TV, and debates had positioned him as one of the leading voices in promoting Islamic knowledge and interfaith harmony.
2016 - Faces a ban in several countries over allegations
In 2016, Dr. Naik came under scrutiny when allegations surfaced that some of his speeches promoted religious extremism. Following these accusations, the Indian government banned his organization, IRF, and revoked his passport. Several countries, including India, Bangladesh, and Canada, also banned his entry. However, Dr. Naik denied these claims, asserting that his lectures were focused on peace and dialogue, and any allegations of extremism were unfounded.
2017 - Relocates to Malaysia
Amid increasing political and legal challenges in India, Dr. Naik relocated to Malaysia in 2017, where he was granted permanent residency. From Malaysia, he continued his outreach efforts, delivering lectures and producing content via online platforms. His supporters argue that Dr. Naik has been a proponent of peaceful interfaith dialogue, while his detractors maintain concerns about some of his messages.
2020 - Continues digital outreach
With the growing influence of digital platforms, Dr. Naik's lectures and videos became widely available on YouTube and social media. Despite the controversies, his content remained popular among certain groups, and he continued to speak about Islam, education, and comparative religion through these channels.

Comparative Religion -- Dr. Zakir Naik's Key Focus

Comparative religion is a field of study that examines the similarities and differences between the world's major religions. Dr. Naik specialized in this area, particularly focusing on the common principles between Islam, Christianity, Hinduism, Judaism, and other faiths. His approach involved quoting scriptures from the Bible, the Bhagavad Gita, the Torah, and the Qur'an to highlight shared values, ethical guidelines, and spiritual teachings. He often stressed that while the specifics of religious practice may differ, the core messages of faiths often align around concepts like monotheism, morality, and justice.

Dr. Naik's goal with comparative religion was to foster greater understanding and reduce misunderstandings between followers of different religions. He believed that by focusing on common ground, interfaith harmony could be achieved, and people of different beliefs could coexist peacefully.
